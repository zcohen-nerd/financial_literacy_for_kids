Many children grow up without understanding how money actually works.

They know you use money to buy things. They see adults swipe cards and tap phones. But they rarely learn **how financial systems work** or how to **make thoughtful decisions about money**.

That realization is why I built this curriculum.

<!-- truncate -->

---

## The Problem I Kept Seeing

Kids grow up surrounded by money — allowances, stores, online purchases, ads — but they often don't understand:

- how saving works
- how spending choices add up
- how earning money connects to work and value
- how to tell the difference between needs and wants
- how financial systems shape everyday life

Even more importantly: many kids felt **anxious or confused about money**, despite encountering it every day.

They knew how to ask for things.

They didn't know how to **think about money**.

That gap needed to be addressed.

---

## What I Wanted to Build

I wanted a curriculum that:

- treats money as a **tool for thinking and decision-making**
- builds **confidence and curiosity** instead of anxiety
- helps kids develop **their own reasoning** about financial choices
- moves beyond simple rules into **real understanding**

The result is this 18-week program.

---

## What Makes This Different

This isn't a curriculum about memorizing definitions or following rigid rules.

It's about helping kids build **mental models** for how money works — so they can figure things out for themselves.

Every lesson is designed to be:

- hands-on
- discussion-driven
- reflective
- built around real-world scenarios that kids can relate to

---

## The Goal

By the end of the program, students should feel like money is something they can **understand, manage, and use wisely**.

That's the whole point.
